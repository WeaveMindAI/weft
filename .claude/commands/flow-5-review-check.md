description: Paranoid red-team pass over the fixes made during review, re-verifying each one
---

This is the paranoid pass. The review rounds found bugs and fixed them, and experience says the fixes themselves are the most likely source of NEW bugs. I'm running this as its own dedicated pass on purpose: when you review and fix in one go you tend to stop being obsessive about the fixes, but coming back fresh to scrutinize them, you can be genuinely less lenient with yourself. Switch to [red team mode]. Be paranoid. Default to "this fix is probably hiding something" until you've proven otherwise.

Focus on the fixes and reshapes made since we started reviewing this feature, plus anything they touched, and for each one assume the worst and try to disprove it. Did it introduce a new bug? Trace the new code paths, what breaks at the edges, what did the reshape's blast radius touch that we never re-checked? Is it actually clean, or secretly dirty? No dirty fallback, no silent error recovery, no dirty patch dressed up as a fix, no spaghetti, no "for now", and if any fix is dirty then it's not done, redo it as the right shape. Did a fix walk back a deliberate past decision? Across many rounds a "fix" can quietly revert something we settled earlier for a real reason, so if a fix changed the shape, confirm that shape wasn't a past intentional choice before you trust it. Don't ping-pong the architecture. Per [red team mode], after each fix you assess, output "counter-perspective:" and actively hunt for how it could be wrong, look for the blind spot, not the confirmation.

Don't trust your own eyes alone on this. Launch review agents to adversarially verify the fixes, in parallel, in a single message with multiple Agent tool calls, all of them the `code-reviewer` agent type, never sequentially. They run on Fable, which is sharp at finding the hole a fix is hiding. Scope them to cover all the fixes with no gaps, and pass each agent the project rules it needs since they don't share your CLAUDE.md or MEMORY.md. Here their job is to REFUTE each fix, not bless it and not redesign it: prove the fix is dirty, incomplete, or introduces a new bug. When an agent does propose a better shape, treat it as you would in review, an input you judge against the whole architecture, since you hold the full rules and it holds a slice. Validate what they return, an agent may flag a deliberate choice it lacked context for, so confirm realness before acting.

Keep going, round after round, until a pass surfaces nothing new and real. Don't stop at the first clean-looking result, the tail is where the surprises hide. It does not matter how many back-and-forths or how long it takes, no shortcuts. When a fix turns out dirty or buggy, fix it cleanly (reshape not patch, DRY, fail loudly never silently) and re-verify it with an agent before you call it settled.

When you're truly satisfied that every fix is clean and bug-free and nothing new is surfacing, tell me, with a short account of what you re-verified and what, if anything, you had to redo.
